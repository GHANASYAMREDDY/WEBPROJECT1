About Mango Crop

Growing Conditions:
Climate: Mango grows best in tropical and subtropical climates with temperatures between 24°C and 30°C.
Rainfall: Requires 75–250 cm annual rainfall but cannot tolerate waterlogging.
Sunlight: Needs full sunlight for fruit development.
Soil: Prefers well-drained loamy soil with good organic matter. pH 5.5–7.5 ideal.

Varieties:
Alfanso, Banganapalli, Dasheri, Kesar, Langra, Totapuri, Neelam, Mallika, Amrapali.

Planting Season:
Best season: June–July (monsoon).
In irrigated areas, planting can be done from February–April.

Planting Material:
Grafted mango plants are recommended for better yield and disease resistance.

Spacing:
Traditional: 8m × 8m
High-density: 4m × 2m for dwarf varieties like Amrapali.

Irrigation:
Mango plants require irrigation every 10–12 days during the fruiting season.
Avoid irrigation during flowering to prevent flower drop.

Fertilizers:
FYM/Compost: 20–25 kg per plant per year.
NPK: 500g N, 200g P, 300g K for young plants;
Mature trees require 1–1.5 kg N, 600g P, and 1 kg K per plant annually.
Apply micronutrients like Zinc and Boron for improved fruit quality.

Pests:
Mango hopper, fruit fly, stem borer, mealy bug.
Use traps and recommended sprays.

Diseases:
Powdery mildew, anthracnose, bacterial canker.
Use resistant varieties and proper orchard hygiene.

Intercultural Operations:
Pruning, mulching, weeding, and soil loosening help improve fruit growth.

Flowering & Fruiting:
Mango trees flower once a year, mostly in January–March.
Fruits mature in April–June depending on the variety.

Harvesting:
Harvest when fruits are fully mature but still firm.
Avoid harvesting immediately after rain.

Yield:
A well-managed mango tree can yield 200–400 fruits per year.
High-density orchards produce 10–12 tons per acre.
