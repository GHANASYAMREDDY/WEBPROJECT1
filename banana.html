About Banana Crop

Growing Conditions:
Climate: Banana grows best in warm, humid tropical climates. Ideal temperature: 20°C–30°C.
Rainfall: Requires 100–150 cm annual rainfall or regular irrigation.
Sunlight: Requires full sunlight for better growth and fruiting.
Soil: Prefers deep, well-drained loamy soil rich in organic matter. Avoid saline soils.

Planting Season:
Main Season: June–July (monsoon).
Other Seasons: February–March and October–November, depending on irrigation availability.

Planting Material:
Rhizomes (suckers) or tissue culture (TC) plants.
TC plants provide uniform growth and high yield.

Spacing:
6 ft × 6 ft or 7 ft × 5 ft depending on variety and soil fertility.

Irrigation:
Requires water every 7–10 days in winter and every 4–5 days in summer.
Avoid waterlogging, which damages roots.

Fertilizers:
FYM/Compost: 10–15 tons per acre.
NPK: 100–150 kg Nitrogen, 60 kg Phosphorus, 60 kg Potassium per acre.
Apply nitrogen in 3 split doses.
Use micronutrients like Zinc and Magnesium for healthy growth.

Pests:
Banana weevil, aphids, and nematodes.
Use organic and recommended chemical treatments as needed.

Diseases:
Panama wilt, Sigatoka leaf spot, and bunchy top virus.
Use disease-resistant varieties and maintain field hygiene.

Intercultural Operations:
Desuckering, mulching, propping, and pruning improve yield quality.

Harvesting:
Harvest when fruits are mature, green, and full-sized.
Harvesting time: 10–12 months after planting depending on the variety.

Yield:
Good yield ranges from 20–30 tons per acre with proper management.
