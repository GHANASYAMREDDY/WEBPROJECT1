About Mirchi Crop

Growing Conditions:
Climate: Warm and humid climate; ideal temperature 20°C – 30°C.
Rainfall: Requires 60–90 cm rainfall; excess rain reduces quality.
Soil: Well-drained loamy soil with pH 6.0–7.0.
Sunlight: Requires 6–8 hours full sunlight daily.

Sowing & Harvesting:
Sowing (Kharif): June – July.
Sowing (Rabi): October – November.
Harvesting: First picking after 70–90 days of transplanting.

Important Processes:
Drying: Fresh chillies dried in sunlight for 7–10 days.
Grading: Sorted by size, colour & pungency.
Processing: Used for chilli powder, flakes, sauces.

Key Facts:
India is one of the largest chilli producers & exporters.
Rich in Vitamins A & C.
Popular varieties: Guntur, Byadagi, Teja, 334.
Major pests include Thrips & Mites.
