About Tomato Crop

Growing conditions:
Climate: Tomatoes require warm weather with temperatures between 20°C and 30°C. Cannot tolerate frost.
Soil: Prefers well-drained loamy soil rich in organic matter. pH 6.0–7.0 is ideal.
Sowing Seasons: Kharif (June–July), Rabi (Oct–Nov), Summer (Jan–Feb).

Sowing & Planting:
Seed Rate: 100–150 grams per acre.
Transplanting: Seedlings are transplanted after 25–30 days.
Spacing: 3 ft between rows, 1.5 ft between plants.

Irrigation:
First irrigation immediately after transplanting, then every 5–7 days. Avoid water stagnation.

Fertilizers:
Apply 8–10 tons FYM/compost per acre.
NPK: 50 kg Nitrogen, 25 kg Phosphorus, 25 kg Potassium in split doses.

Pests:
Fruit borer, whitefly, and aphids are common. Use pheromone traps and recommended sprays.

Diseases:
Early blight, late blight, and wilt. Use disease-resistant varieties and fungicides.

Harvesting:
Harvest when fruits turn light red or red. Picking is done every 3–4 days.

Yield:
Average yield is 8–12 tons per acre under good management.
