About Sweet Corn Crop

Growing Conditions:
Climate: Sweet corn grows best in warm climates with temperatures between 18°C and 30°C.
Sunlight: Requires full sunlight for healthy cob development.
Soil: Prefers well-drained loamy soil rich in organic matter. pH 5.5–7.0 ideal.

Sowing Season:
Kharif: June – July
Rabi: October – November
Summer: January – February

Sowing & Planting:
Seed Rate: 4–5 kg per acre.
Spacing: Row spacing 2.5 ft and plant spacing 1 ft.
Seeds are sown directly in the field.

Irrigation:
First irrigation immediately after sowing.
Subsequent irrigation every 6–7 days.
Avoid waterlogging during early growth stages.

Fertilizers:
FYM/Compost: 8–10 tons per acre.
NPK: 60 kg Nitrogen, 30 kg Phosphorus, 30 kg Potassium.
Apply nitrogen in 2–3 split doses.

Pests:
Stem borer, fall armyworm, and aphids commonly attack sweet corn.
Use recommended pest control measures and pheromone traps.

Diseases:
Leaf blight, rust, and downy mildew.
Use resistant varieties and maintain proper spacing.

Harvesting:
Harvest when kernels are soft, milky, and fully filled.
Generally harvested 70–90 days after sowing.

Yield:
Good yield ranges from 15–20 quintals per acre depending on management.
